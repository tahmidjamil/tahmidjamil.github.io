Syed Tahmid Jamil

Lecturer & Researcher · Industrial Engineering · Dhaka, Bangladesh

I am a Lecturer in the Department of Industrial Engineering at BGMEA University of Fashion and Technology (BUFT), Dhaka. My research sits at the intersection of ergonomics, data science, and quality engineering — with a focus on real-world problems in Bangladesh's manufacturing sector, particularly the ready-made garment (RMG) industry.

I am currently completing an MSc in Applied Statistics and Data Science at Jahangirnagar University, where I apply time-series forecasting and Bayesian modelling methods to industrial and supply-chain challenges. I am passionate about improving worker safety and operational efficiency through evidence-based engineering.

Teaching

Courses taught at BGMEA University of Fashion and Technology (BUFT), Dhaka.

As a Lecturer in the Department of Industrial Engineering at BUFT, I am committed to connecting theoretical foundations with hands-on, industry-relevant applications. My teaching philosophy emphasises problem-solving, quantitative rigour, and real-world case studies drawn from Bangladesh's manufacturing sector.

Current Courses
IE 4203

Simulation

An upper-division course covering discrete-event simulation methodologies and their application in industrial systems. Topics include Monte Carlo simulation, random number generation, probability distributions, model validation, and simulation software. Students work on hands-on projects modelling real manufacturing and service systems to analyse performance and support decision-making.

Monte CarloDiscrete-Event SimulationStatistical ModellingPython / Arena
IE 3203

Material Handling and Maintenance Management

A core course addressing the principles of material flow, storage systems, and equipment selection in manufacturing and logistics environments. The maintenance management component covers preventive, predictive, and corrective maintenance strategies, reliability analysis, and total productive maintenance (TPM). Emphasis is placed on applications relevant to Bangladesh's RMG and textile industries.

Material FlowTPMReliabilityLogistics
Teaching Philosophy

I believe effective engineering education requires bridging the gap between mathematical abstraction and real industrial problems. In my courses I:

  • Use local industry case studies (RMG, footwear, food supply chains) to contextualise theory.
  • Integrate programming tools (Python, R, simulation software) from the first week.
  • Encourage students to question assumptions and interpret results critically.
  • Maintain an open-door policy for consultation and research mentorship.
